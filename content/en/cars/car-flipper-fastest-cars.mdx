To optimize your tuning workflow and avoid wasting valuable car parts, follow this structured upgrade sequence.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Complete Full Structural Repairs">
    Before installing any performance upgrades, ensure the vehicle is at 100% repair status. Tuning a damaged chassis reduces the efficiency of the upgraded parts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Install Stage-Three Engine Blocks">
    The engine block serves as the foundation for your top speed. Always prioritize upgrading the engine before fine-tuning the gearbox or weight distribution.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Optimize Gear Ratios">
    Equip a high-tier gearbox to ensure your vehicle can utilize its increased horsepower without hitting a premature RPM ceiling on straightaways.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Equip Performance Tires">
    High top speed is useless without traction. Upgrading your tires ensures that power is successfully transferred to the road, preventing spinouts during acceleration.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Tuning Warning">
Avoid installing cosmetic modifications like heavy body kits before performance upgrades if your primary goal is speed. Some cosmetic items can alter the vehicle's center of mass, slightly impacting cornering stability.
</Callout>
