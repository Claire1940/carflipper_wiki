<Section icon={Wrench} title="Tuning Shop Optimization Guide">

The Tuning Shop update transformed the **car flipper keep or sell** decision-making process. Performance parts now allow you to take a mediocre car and turn it into a high-value asset. However, the order in which you apply these upgrades determines your efficiency.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Full Restoration">
    Before touching the Tuning Shop, ensure the car is at 100% health. You cannot tune a broken engine effectively.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Define the Purpose">
    Decide immediately: is this a flip or a keeper? This prevents wasting expensive parts on a car you plan to sell in five minutes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Aesthetic Foundation">
    Apply body kits and paint. This sets the "soul" of the car and helps you decide if the look is worth keeping.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Performance Layering">
    Install performance parts starting with the engine, then tires, then suspension. This provides the most noticeable driving improvement.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Performance parts are often obtained through containers. Using codes like `3KLIKES` or `RELEASE` can give you a head start with Rare or Uncommon parts. These parts are best saved for cars you intend to keep, as their resale value boost doesn't always cover their rarity value in the early game.

<Callout type="info" title="Part Management">
Common car parts are plentiful and should be used on every flip. Rare and Legendary parts should be hoarded for your top-tier S-class collection cars.
</Callout>

| Part Rarity | Acquisition | Usage Strategy | Sell Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Common** | Containers/Scrap | Use on every flip | Minimal |
| **Uncommon** | Containers | Use on mid-tier flips | Moderate |
| **Rare** | Codes/High-end Containers | Save for collection | High |
| **Legendary** | Rare Drops | **Never sell** | Extreme |

</Section>

To fund these expansions, you must develop a "Profit Path." This involves identifying which cars in the current 100+ vehicle roster have the best "buy-to-sell" margin. Generally, cars with heavy body damage but intact engines offer the best ROI, as bodywork is cheaper to fix than mechanical components.

<Callout type="success" title="Empire Building">
Once you reach the Elite Workshop tier, start a "One-In, One-Out" policy. Only keep a new car if it is better than one of your existing collection pieces. Sell the weaker one to fund the new build.
