<Section icon={Target} title="Performance Parts: Core Car Flipper Upgrades Explained">

To maximize the value of your vehicles, you must understand the different upgrade categories available in the Tuning Shop. Upgrades are divided into structural repairs, visual customization, and mechanical performance parts. Each category serves a specific purpose in the vehicle-restoration loop and affects either the car's resale value, its driving physics, or both.

Applying the right **car flipper upgrades** requires balancing your current budget with the vehicle's potential. For example, installing expensive racing tires on a common starter car is rarely cost-effective. Instead, match the tier of the parts to the rarity of the vehicle.

| Upgrade Category | Primary Role | Best Used For | Value Impact | Recommended Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Restore & Repair** | Fixes broken chassis and engine blocks | Every single project car | High baseline increase | Maximum priority |
| **Customization Options** | Alters visual body kits, spoilers, and rims | Showcase builds and high-tier flips | Moderate visual appeal | Medium priority |
| **Performance Parts** | Enhances top speed, acceleration, and grip | Racing cars and personal collection | High performance boost | High for kept cars |
| **Personalization** | Adjusts paint finishes, neon underglow, and tints | Customizing signature vehicles | Low profit impact | Low priority |
| **Workshop Expansion** | Unlocks advanced bays and faster tools | Long-term progression goals | Multiplies overall output | Maximum priority |

<Callout type="info" title="Repair Foundation">
Never install performance parts on a car that has unresolved structural damage. The game calculates resale profit based on the vehicle's overall condition, and upgrading a broken engine block yields diminished financial returns.
</Callout>
