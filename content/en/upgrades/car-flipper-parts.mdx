### Understanding Part Rarity and Allocation

Before diving into complex tuning, you must understand where your parts come from and how to use them efficiently. Using rare parts on a low-tier starter vehicle is a common mistake that can severely stunt your financial growth.

| Part Rarity | Primary Source | Usage Focus | Value Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Common** | Common Containers, Starter Codes | Basic repairs, Starter flips | Low-Medium increase |
| **Uncommon** | Uncommon Containers, Active Codes | Mid-tier tuning, Performance | Balanced boost |
| **Rare** | Rare Containers, Milestones | High-end builds, Dream collection | Massive multiplier |

<Callout type="info" title="Resource Management">
Always hoard your Rare parts during the first few hours of gameplay. Focus on using Common and Uncommon parts to flip starter vehicles until you expand your workshop and unlock high-tier cars that warrant premium upgrades.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Performance Upgrades: Tuning Shop Integration">

The Tuning Shop update introduces an intricate layer of customization to the traditional repair-and-sell loop. Once a vehicle is fully restored to a functional state, the Tuning Shop allows you to install performance parts that directly modify the vehicle's driving physics, top speed, acceleration, and handling characteristics. This is particularly important if you plan to use your vehicles for racing friends or exploring the massive open world, rather than immediately selling them for profit.

Upgrading your vehicle is not merely about aesthetic appeal; it is about statistical optimization. Each part installed contributes to the vehicle's overall performance rating. However, because customization options are vast, you must tailor your upgrades to the specific role of the vehicle. A dedicated drift car requires different tuning parameters compared to a straight-line drag racer or a balanced daily driver.

### Upgrade Categories and Statistical Impact

To get the most out of your inventory, prioritize upgrades based on the vehicle's intended purpose. The table below outlines the primary upgrade categories available in the Tuning Shop and their direct impact on vehicle performance.

| Upgrade Area | Required Parts | Performance Boost | Best Target |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Engine Tuning** | High-tier parts | Maximum top speed, Acceleration | Racing builds |
| **Suspension** | Mid-tier parts | Handling, Drift control, Stability | World exploration |
| **Body Aesthetics** | Visual components | Overall presentation, Resale value | Profit flips |
| **Tires & Grip** | Balanced parts | Traction control, Launch acceleration | Track racing |

<Callout type="tip" title="Upgrade Priority">
If your primary goal is racing friends, prioritize Engine Tuning and Tires. If your goal is maximizing resale profit, focus on visual customization and basic restoration, as aesthetic completeness heavily influences buyer payout.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Map} title="Step-by-Step Upgrade and Restoration Workflow">

Restoring a vehicle from a rusty wreck to a tuned masterpiece requires a systematic approach. Skipping steps or installing high-end performance modifications on a structurally damaged frame will result in wasted resources and diminished financial returns. Follow this proven workshop workflow to ensure maximum efficiency for every vehicle that enters your bay.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Acquisition and Inspection">
    Purchase a damaged vehicle from the salvage yard or auction house. Inspect the frame and identify which base components are broken or missing before spending any parts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Structural and Mechanical Repair">
    Use your Common and Uncommon parts to repair core mechanical issues. The vehicle must be fully functional and drivable before any cosmetic or performance tuning can begin.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Visual Customization">
    Apply paint, body kits, and custom wheels. Utilize the game's hundreds of customization options to create a cohesive visual identity that increases the vehicle's overall presentation value.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Performance Tuning">
    Take the vehicle to the Tuning Shop. Install high-tier engine, suspension, and exhaust parts to boost the vehicle's stats, preparing it for high-stakes racing or premium collection status.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Final Evaluation: Keep or Sell">
    Assess the completed build. Decide whether to keep the vehicle in your personal garage showcase, use it to race friends, or sell it to secure the capital needed for workshop expansion.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Repair Order Matters">
Never install premium performance upgrades on a vehicle that still has unresolved structural damage. The game calculates value based on completeness, and tuning a broken car yields significantly lower progression efficiency.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Workshop Expansion and Parts Management">

As your car empire grows, your starting garage will quickly become insufficient. Expanding your workshop is vital to unlock more vehicle bays, access advanced tuning tools, and increase your overall storage capacity for spare parts and containers. Managing your workshop's economy requires a careful balance between the capital spent on expansion and the resources invested in individual vehicle builds.

To maintain a healthy progression rate, set clear milestones for your workshop. Do not rush to buy expensive vehicles if you do not have the spare parts inventory required to restore them. A high-tier vehicle sitting in your bay completely stripped of parts generates zero income and actively blocks your workshop's earning potential.

### Keep vs. Sell Decision Matrix
