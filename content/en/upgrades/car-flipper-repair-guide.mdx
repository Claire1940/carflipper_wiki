<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Car Flipper Repair Guide: Step-by-Step Restoration">

Restoring a vehicle requires a systematic approach to ensure you do not waste valuable resources or overlook hidden defects. Follow this step-by-step **car flipper repair guide** to maximize your workshop's efficiency and ensure every vehicle leaves your bay in pristine condition.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Acquire the Vehicle">
    Purchase a damaged car from the market or retrieve a project vehicle from your inventory. Move it to an open repair bay in your workshop to begin the inspection.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Inspect and Strip">
    Examine the vehicle to identify broken components, rust patches, and missing parts. Remove damaged body panels and worn-out mechanical parts to prepare the chassis.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Perform Mechanical Repairs">
    Address the engine, suspension, and drivetrain first. Replace broken parts with new components. A functional engine is the foundation of a high-value vehicle.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Bodywork and Paint">
    Fix structural damage, remove rust, and install new body panels. Apply a fresh coat of paint and customize the exterior styling to improve the vehicle's visual appeal.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Tuning and Upgrades">
    Take the vehicle to the Tuning Shop. Install performance parts and fine-tune the engine to boost horsepower, handling, and overall performance rating.
  </Step>
  <Step num="6" title="Final Evaluation">
    Review the completed build. Decide whether to list the restored vehicle on the market for a massive profit or add it to your permanent collection.
  </Step>
</Steps>

To keep your workshop running smoothly, use the priority system below when managing your resources during a restoration project.

| Repair Phase | Action Required | Cost Level | Value Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1: Mechanical** | Engine, drivetrain, suspension | High | Essential |
| **Phase 2: Structural** | Rust removal, panel replacement | Medium | High |
| **Phase 3: Cosmetic** | Paint, wheels, interior styling | Low | Medium |
| **Phase 4: Tuning** | Performance parts, ECU tuning | Very High | Optional |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Repair Habit">
Always complete rust removal before applying paint. Painting over rust reduces the final quality score of the vehicle, lowering its maximum resale value.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Tuning Shop Upgrades and Customization">

The Tuning Shop is where you transform a standard restored car into a high-performance machine. With hundreds of customization options available, you can personalize every aspect of your vehicle, from cosmetic body kits to high-end turbochargers and suspension kits.

Tuning should be treated as an investment. If you are repairing a car purely for a quick profit, keep the tuning minimal, as buyers may not always pay a premium for top-tier performance parts on a starter chassis. However, if you are building a vehicle for racing or your personal collection, maximizing performance is highly recommended.

| Upgrade Category | Primary Benefit | Cost | Priority for Flips |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Engine Blocks** | Massive horsepower boost | Very High | Low |
| **Suspension Kits** | Improved handling and drift control | Medium | Medium |
| **Body Kits** | Enhanced visual rating and style | Low | High |
| **Tires & Rims** | Better grip and customized look | Medium | Medium |
| **Turbochargers** | Extreme acceleration increase | High | Low |

<Callout type="info" title="Tuning Strategy">
Visual upgrades like body kits and custom paint offer the highest return on investment (ROI) for low-tier cars. Save expensive engine upgrades for rare and legendary vehicles.
</Callout>
