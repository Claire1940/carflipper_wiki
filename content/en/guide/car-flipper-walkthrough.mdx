<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Car Restoration Loop">

To maximize your profit margins, you must follow a disciplined restoration sequence. Skipping steps or upgrading a vehicle out of order will drain your bank account and leave you with unfinished inventory. The restoration process is divided into five distinct phases, moving your project car systematically from a rusty wreck to a high-performance masterpiece.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Acquire a Damaged Vehicle">
    Browse the local salvage listings or auction block to find a suitable project car. Look for vehicles with clean titles but heavily damaged mechanical parts, as these offer the highest potential profit margins once fully restored.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Perform Diagnostic Checks">
    Bring the vehicle to your active repair bay. Use your diagnostic tools to scan the engine block, transmission, and suspension. Identify every broken, rusted, or missing part before purchasing replacement components.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Execute Core Mechanical Repairs">
    Replace all faulty internal parts. Ensure the engine runs smoothly and the chassis is structurally sound. A vehicle must be fully functional mechanically before any aesthetic or performance modifications can be successfully applied.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Apply Visual Customization">
    Head to the paint booth and body workshop. Apply fresh coats of paint, install custom body kits, and replace stock wheels with premium rims. Visual appeal directly increases the base multiplier of the car's final valuation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Install Performance Tuning Parts">
    Equip high-tier turbos, sport exhausts, and racing suspensions. These upgrades prepare the vehicle for high-speed exploration or make it highly attractive to high-end collectors looking for turn-key racing builds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Over-Upgrading Starter Cars">
It is highly recommended to avoid installing expensive performance parts on common, low-tier starter vehicles. Common cars have a lower maximum valuation cap, meaning you will rarely recoup the cost of high-tier turbos or racing engines on a basic sedan. Save your premium parts for rare and legendary chassis.
</Callout>
