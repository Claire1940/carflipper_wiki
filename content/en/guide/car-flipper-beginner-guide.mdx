<Section icon={Shield} title="Sourcing and Repairing Your First Wrecks">

Sourcing the right project car is the most important decision you make in Car Flipper. If you pay too much for a damaged vehicle, your profit margins will shrink, regardless of how well you restore it. Look for vehicles with manageable damage levels that fit your current toolset. In the early game, stick to common commuter cars and basic sports models, as their replacement parts are inexpensive and easily accessible.

Once a vehicle is secured in your workshop, the mechanical restoration begins. You must systematically diagnose and address issues across the chassis, engine bay, and suspension. Skipping minor repairs might save you a few credits upfront, but it severely penalizes the vehicle's final appraisal value when you attempt to flip it.

| Damage Severity | Common Symptoms | Average Repair Cost | Value Recovery | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Minor** | Scratched paint, missing lights, flat tires | Low | High | Buy immediately for quick turnarounds |
| **Moderate** | Dented panels, worn brake pads, radiator leaks | Medium | Steady | Ideal project car for steady progression |
| **Major** | Cracked engine block, bent frame, blown suspension | High | Variable | Avoid until your workshop is fully upgraded |

To ensure you do not miss any critical steps during your first restoration, follow this structured repair sequence to get your project car back into peak condition.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Secure the Salvage">
    Purchase a damaged vehicle from the marketplace, ensuring the initial cost leaves you with enough reserve cash to buy replacement parts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Run Diagnostics">
    Interact with your workshop terminal to scan the vehicle. Identify which parts are completely broken versus those that simply need a cosmetic polish.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Execute Mechanical Repairs">
    Replace internal engine components, fix the exhaust, and restore the brake assemblies. Always prioritize functional repairs over visual styling.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Restore the Bodywork">
    Use your workshop tools to pull out dents, replace missing panels, and apply a fresh primer coat to prepare the exterior for the paint booth.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Warning">
Never list a vehicle for sale if it still has active mechanical faults. Buyers in the marketplace heavily discount cars with unresolved engine or structural issues, which can result in a net financial loss.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Mastering the Tuning Shop and Customization">

The Tuning Shop is where your restored vehicles truly transform. Once a car is mechanically sound, you can dive into hundreds of customization options to elevate its aesthetic appeal and performance. Performance upgrades, on the other hand, directly impact how the car drives in the open world. Installing high-tier turbochargers, sport tires, and upgraded gearboxes will turn a slow starter car into a competitive racer. However, because these performance parts carry a high price tag, you must balance your upgrade budget against your intended outcome.
